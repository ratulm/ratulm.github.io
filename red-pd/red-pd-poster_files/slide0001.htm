RED-PD: RED with Preferential Dropping
Ratul Mahajan        Sally Floyd (ACIRI)        David Wetherall
8RED-PD identifies and controls high bandwidth flows at the congested router to improve fairness.
Spectrum of possibilities
Ø No flow state is simple but implies no protection.
Ø Per-flow state provides protection but is heavyweight in presence of  lots of  “Web mice”.
Ø RED-PD combines simplicity and protection by keeping state only for flows which are high bandwidth. We call this “partial flow state”.
ØBandwidth distribution is heavy tailed. A small fraction of flows account for most of the bandwidth.
ØIn this case, controlling only 10% of the flows will give control over 80% of the bandwidth.
Effect of RED-PD
RED-PD internals
ØDefine target bandwidth T as throughput of a TCP flow with RTT R given drop rate p at the router.
ØUse drop history of length dependent on R and p to identify flows sending more than T. Divide the history into multiple lists to filter out losses in a window of data.
ØPreferentially drop from these flows with a dropping probability based on the flows’ sending rate. Make iterative changes to dropping probability based on p and feedback from identification engine to bring down the flow to T before inserting it into the output queue.
Flows 1-9 are TCPs, 10-12 are high bandwidth CBRs.
